  • Time Your Coffee Wisely

    Coffee Break Online Course
    Charge up between 9:30 a.m. and 11:30 a.m., and between 2 p.m. and 5 p.m. This goes for coffee or your caffeine of choice. Why not early in the morning? The body naturally produces cortisol in the morning, which helps us wake up. Caffeine messes with our cortisol production, making us MORE dependent on caffeine. Make the most of your day by timing your caffeine to complete your goals.

  • Take Down Notes

    You're presumably watching the lecture on your computer as an online student. Don't let the ease of rewinding and replaying the video deter you from taking notes. In fact, put down your laptop and pick up a pen and paper. Taking notes by hand might help you focus and think critically, instead of typing everything out on a computer. You'll retain more information and form a stronger bond with the course.

  • Don't Multitask

    Focus on one assignment at a time and focus on the task at hanD. Arrange your projects in priority order, and focus on the most important things that demand the most work.

    If you're having trouble keeping focused, consider making to-do lists using a project management application like Notion.

  • Take a Rest

    You read that right! Take a rest, sweetheart. Academic tasks often tire us out, and can lead to burnout so it is important to relax once in a while. I know rest seems to be a reproachful activity when we are overloaded with backlogs but it is never a bad thing to take a break, and allow yourself to reset.

    Your rest may include watching k-drama, or painting, or simply taking a nap! Just do you, and chill for a while.

  • Try Review Techniques

    Want to be efficient in studying? Try review techniques like the Pomodoro Method where you deep study for 25 minutes without distractions then rest for the next 5 minutes. With this, you are able to rest without waiting for a long time before giving yourself a break! Also, this could help preventing burnout.

    I highly suggest that you use your 5 minute break after each task by stretching, or drinking water instead of scrolling in your social media account.
